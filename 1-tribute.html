Dr. César Milstein

"Science will only fulfill its promises when the benefits are equally shared by the really poor of the world."

Here's a brief timeline of César Milsatein's scientific career:

  • 1927: Born in Argentina
  • 1958: Joined the Biochemistry Department at the University of Cambridge
  • 1975: Elected Fellow of the Royal Soceity
  • 1980: Awarded the Louisa Gross Horwitz Prize
  • 1980: Awarded the Wolf Prize in Medicine
  • 1981: Awarded the William Bate Hardy Prize
  • 1981: Awarded the Sir Hans Krebs Medal
  • 1982: Awarded the Franklin Medal
  • 1984: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine
  • 1989: Awarded the Copley Medal
  • 1993: Awarded the Diamond Knoex Award of Argentina
  • 2002: Died in Cambridge, England, UK
  • 2010: The documentary "Un fueguito, la historia de César Milstein" released
